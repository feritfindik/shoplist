What It Is

A compact, battery-powered rechargeable inflator with a 4500mAh (14.8V / ~66Wh) battery, brushless high-speed motor (advertised 80,000 r/min) and dual-stage inflation capable of up to 20 PSI. It weighs ~900 g, measures about 143 x 137 x 67.5 mm, charges via USB-C (QC3.1 supported), and has auto-off at set PSI plus an auto-off deflate function.

Why Buy

Extremely portable and truly cordless — it fits in a small dry bag and recharges, letting you inflate/deflate SUPs, kayaks, tents and wings without access to mains or a car. The brushless motor and dual-stage design deliver fast initial volume then a high-pressure finish, saving time and effort compared with hand pumps and larger corded inflators.

Why Not Buy

Not ideal if you routinely inflate large high-pressure boards (12'6" or very frequent 20 PSI sessions) because the small unit runs hot in sustained high-pressure use and long-term durability under heavy high-PSI duty is a concern. Also it does not include a wall charger and can be quite loud in its high-pressure/second stage.

Usage Tips
Use the dual-stage workflow: start on low/high-volume to quickly get bulk inflation, then switch to high-pressure to finish to target PSI and let auto-off stop the pump.
Bring a 30W (or higher) USB-C PD charger for reasonable recharge times; low-power phone chargers will charge extremely slowly.
If the included gaskets don’t seal your valve, try the gasket from your hand pump or try the set of provided washers to improve the seal.
Avoid repeatedly inflating large 12'6"/20 PSI boards back-to-back — allow cool-down time between high-pressure runs to reduce heat stress.
Use the supplied dry bag to carry and keep the pump dry on the water; you can clip/hang the pump while it runs to reduce perceived noise.
Add a simple inlet filter if you plan to operate on sandy beaches to reduce debris intake (some users reported doing this).
Tighten adapters fully before inflating — some users found certain fittings require careful seating and a firm screw/lock to avoid leaks.
