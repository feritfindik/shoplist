Best for truly portable, cordless SUP inflation and quick day trips.

What it is

A compact, battery-powered rechargeable inflator with a 4500mAh (14.8V / ~66Wh) battery, brushless high-speed motor (advertised 80,000 r/min) and dual-stage inflation capable of up to 20 PSI. It weighs ~900 g, measures about 143 x 137 x 67.5 mm, charges via USB-C (QC3.1 supported), and has auto-off at set PSI plus an auto-off deflate function.

Why Buy

Extremely portable and truly cordless — it fits in a small dry bag and recharges, letting you inflate/deflate SUPs, kayaks, tents and wings without access to mains or a car. The brushless motor and dual-stage design deliver fast initial volume then a high-pressure finish, saving time and effort compared with hand pumps and larger corded inflators.

Why Not Buy

Not ideal if you routinely inflate large high-pressure boards (12'6" or very frequent 20 PSI sessions) because the small unit runs hot in sustained high-pressure use and long-term durability under heavy high-PSI duty is a concern. Also it does not include a wall charger and can be quite loud in its high-pressure/second stage.

Best for: ultralight, cordless SUP inflation (10'–11' boards up to ~15 PSI)

The Pulse Pro shines when you want a truly portable, throw‑in‑the‑bag pump that can inflate typical all‑around boards quickly, then auto‑shutoff and deflate when you're done. Compared with plug‑in 12V pumps, it trades sustained high‑PSI durability for freedom from cables and car sockets. Versus bigger units like Outdoor Master Shark 2/3, it's dramatically smaller and easier to carry; the Sharks handle repeated 20 PSI sessions better. Against older minis (Cachalot 2, Puffer Pro), the Pulse Pro is often faster and quieter in stage 1 but, like all tiny compressors, gets loud and hot during the high‑pressure phase.

Usage tips

  • Use the dual‑stage workflow: bulk fill first, then high‑pressure to your target PSI (auto‑off will stop the pump).
  • Bring a 30W+ USB‑C PD charger for reasonable recharge times; low‑power phone bricks charge very slowly.
  • If seals leak, try the gasket from your hand pump or the extra washers included.
  • Avoid back‑to‑back 20 PSI inflations on large 12'6″ boards — let the unit cool to reduce heat stress.
  • Use the dry bag to carry and keep the pump dry; you can clip/hang it while running to reduce perceived noise.
  • Add a simple inlet filter for sandy beaches to keep debris out.
  • Tighten adapters fully; some fittings need a firm seat to avoid leaks.
