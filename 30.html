What It Is

A compact, battery-powered rechargeable inflator with a 4500mAh (14.8V / ~66Wh) battery, brushless high-speed motor (advertised 80,000 r/min) and dual-stage inflation capable of up to 20 PSI. It weighs ~900 g, measures about 143 x 137 x 67.5 mm, charges via USB-C (QC3.1 supported), and has auto-off at set PSI plus an auto-off deflate function.

Why Buy

Extremely portable and truly cordless — it fits in a small dry bag and recharges, letting you inflate/deflate SUPs, kayaks, tents and wings without access to mains or a car. The brushless motor and dual-stage design deliver fast initial volume then a high-pressure finish, saving time and effort compared with hand pumps and larger corded inflators.

Why Not Buy

Not ideal if you routinely inflate large high-pressure boards (12'6" or very frequent 20 PSI sessions) because the small unit runs hot in sustained high-pressure use and long-term durability under heavy high-PSI duty is a concern. Also it does not include a wall charger and can be quite loud in its high-pressure/second stage.

Customer Voice

Truly compact and portable

Users consistently praise the Pulse Pro's tiny size (about 900 g, palm-sized) and waterproof carry bag, making it easy to carry to shore, pack in a SUP bungee or stow in a backpack. Many reviewers say it replaces larger, corded pumps for day trips and hikes.

“This incredibly compact battery powered unit filled my SUP from flat to full 12 PSI in 7 minutes ... The size is really good. It's very compact compared to my other SUP pumps.” — Kevin Photo Amazon

“It's small and lightweight ... it's so easy to take right to the shore with you so you don't have to lug whatever you're inflating from the car to the shore.” — WTI Gina YouTube

Good battery life for multiple inflations

The 4500 mAh battery regularly completed multiple inflations on one charge — reviewers and video tests report 2–4 typical inflations (e.g., three 10'6" boards to ~12–15 PSI) per charge depending on board size and target PSI.

“It charges fully in about 90 minutes, and provides 2 to 3 uses per charge.” — flyteach Amazon

“Three boards at up to 15 PSI... that was three boards on one charge. The battery still got three bars left.” — Pulse Pro hands-on review YouTube

Fast and powerful inflation

The brushless motor and two-stage design let the pump inflate boards quickly (many users report 5–10 minutes to reach typical SUP pressures), making it faster and less effort than hand pumps and convenient compared with larger plug-in pumps for shorter trips.

“It takes about 5 minutes to inflate my paddleboard from zero to 13 PSI.” — Matthew Amazon

“While we were pumping them up at the lake ... they were so impressed with how quick it was and how convenient it was to not have to plug into a car for power.” — WTI Gina YouTube

Loud in high-pressure (second) stage

Many users report the pump becomes noticeably loud when it shifts into the high-pressure/compressor stage — this is an expected tradeoff in small high-pressure pumps but can be disruptive at lakes or busy launch spots.

“This worked very quickly and quietly until the SUP ... reached 1 psi. As soon as there was any back pressure the pump changed sounds and got very loud ... it also made the tube very hot!” — D'Anemone Amazon

“It changes sound and goes into high pressure pumping mode.” — Hands-on review YouTube

Heat buildup and strain on heavy high-PSI jobs

Small pumps like this generate heat under sustained high-pressure duty; reviewers and testers warn that repeated inflations at 20 PSI (large/long boards) can overheat connectors and shorten lifespan — one user reported the hose became too hot to touch and ~96 dB at 1 m during use.

“It pumped my 14' SUP in 10.3 minutes and produced 96 decibels at one meter. The hose got so hot I could no longer touch it.” — Amazon Kunde Amazon

“Any of these small Q pumps we've tried ... they all seem to fall apart when you do a 12'6 board that inflates to 20 PSI. ... that seems to be too much heat inside and that seems to kill them.” — Hands-on review YouTube

Some reliability / early-failure reports

While many customers report good performance, isolated reports exist of units failing after a small number of uses (won't charge or turn on), and at least one customer cited poor seller response for warranty/help.

“This worked really well… twice! ... It won’t charge it won’t turn on. I tried contacting the seller with absolutely no response.” — Cindee Amazon

No charger included / slow charging without PD adapter

The pump includes a USB-C cable but not a USB-C power adapter; reviewers emphasize you should bring a 30W+ USB-C PD charger for reasonable recharge times or it will be very slow with low-power phone chargers.

“It doesn't come with a charger. ... You are really gonna want a 30W charger or you need to be 1000% certain that you aren't gonna need to use it more than once a day.” — Jon Allen Amazon

Adapters and sealing can require trial and error

Most users find the supplied stackable nozzles and gaskets cover common valves, but some had to try different gaskets or use a hand-pump gasket to get a reliable seal; fitting certain kite/wing valves may require trial and careful tightening.

“I wasn't getting a good seal with the provided gaskets, and finally used the gasket from the hand pump that came with the board, and it sealed great.” — The Goode Life Outdoors Amazon

“... this also comes with a variety of adapters ... I had to find the one that works.” — Wing inflation how‑to YouTube

Compare Against

  • Outdoor Master Shark 2 / Shark 3: Pulse Pro is far smaller and more portable; faster for single 10'–11' boards. Shark units may handle repeated 20 PSI jobs better due to heat dissipation.
  • Cachalot 2: Pulse Pro is more compact and often quieter in Stage 1, with comparable or faster inflation on mid-size SUPs.
  • Puffer Pro / other mini high‑pressure pumps: Pulse Pro is often smaller yet matches/exceeds speed on 10'–11' boards; like peers, it shares limits on sustained 20 PSI use.
  • Larger plug‑in/12V SUP pumps: Corded pumps suit repeated high‑PSI duty; Pulse Pro trades that for true cordless portability.

Usage Tips

Use the dual-stage workflow: start on low/high-volume to quickly get bulk inflation, then switch to high-pressure to finish to target PSI and let auto-off stop the pump.
Bring a 30W (or higher) USB-C PD charger for reasonable recharge times; low-power phone chargers will charge extremely slowly.
If the included gaskets don’t seal your valve, try the gasket from your hand pump or try the set of provided washers to improve the seal.
Avoid repeatedly inflating large 12'6"/20 PSI boards back-to-back — allow cool-down time between high-pressure runs to reduce heat stress.
Use the supplied dry bag to carry and keep the pump dry on the water; you can clip/hang the pump while it runs to reduce perceived noise.
Add a simple inlet filter if you plan to operate on sandy beaches to reduce debris intake (some users reported doing this).
Tighten adapters fully before inflating — some users found certain fittings require careful seating and a firm screw/lock to avoid leaks.
