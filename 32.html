What It Is

A compact, battery-powered rechargeable inflator with a 4500mAh (14.8V / ~66Wh) battery, brushless high-speed motor (advertised 80,000 r/min) and dual-stage inflation capable of up to 20 PSI. It weighs ~900 g, measures about 143 x 137 x 67.5 mm, charges via USB-C (QC3.1 supported), and has auto-off at set PSI plus an auto-off deflate function.

Why Buy

Extremely portable and truly cordless — it fits in a small dry bag and recharges, letting you inflate/deflate SUPs, kayaks, tents and wings without access to mains or a car. The brushless motor and dual-stage design deliver fast initial volume then a high-pressure finish, saving time and effort compared with hand pumps and larger corded inflators.

Why Not Buy

Not ideal if you routinely inflate large high-pressure boards (12'6" or very frequent 20 PSI sessions) because the small unit runs hot in sustained high-pressure use and long-term durability under heavy high-PSI duty is a concern. Also it does not include a wall charger and can be quite loud in its high-pressure/second stage.

Customer Voice

Compare Against

  • Outdoor Master Shark 2 / Shark 3: The Pulse Pro is far smaller and more portable; it often inflates 10'–11' boards quicker for single trips, while the larger Shark units dissipate heat better for repeated 20 PSI jobs.
  • Cachalot 2: Pulse Pro is more compact and often quieter in Stage 1; delivers comparable or faster inflation for mid-size SUPs.
  • Puffer Pro / other mini pumps: Pulse Pro is smaller than many prior mini pumps but matches/exceeds speed for 10'–11' boards; like others, sustained 20 PSI is the shared limit.
  • Larger plug‑in/12V SUP pumps: Corded pumps can be tougher for heavy, repeated high‑PSI sessions; Pulse Pro wins for cordless convenience and packability.

Usage Tips

  • Use the dual‑stage workflow: bulk fill on low/high‑volume, then switch to high‑pressure to finish at target PSI and let auto‑off stop the pump.
  • Bring a 30W+ USB‑C PD charger for reasonable recharge times; low‑power phone chargers will charge very slowly.
  • If the included gaskets don’t seal, try the gasket from your hand pump or stack the provided washers to improve the seal.
  • Avoid repeatedly inflating large 12'6" / 20 PSI boards back‑to‑back — allow cool‑down between high‑pressure runs.
  • Use the supplied dry bag to carry and keep the pump dry on the water; you can clip/hang the pump while it runs to reduce perceived noise.
  • Add a simple inlet filter for sandy beaches to reduce debris intake.
  • Tighten adapters fully before inflating — some fittings require careful seating and a firm lock to avoid leaks.
